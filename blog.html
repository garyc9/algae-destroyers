Las Vegas windstorms just wrecked your pool — here's what comes next

That recent windstorm blew tons of debris into pools across the valley. Leaves, dust, twigs, you name it. Here's the problem: all that organic matter is now sinking to the bottom, feeding algae. And with temperatures climbing into the 80s this week, you've got a perfect storm for an algae bloom.

What to do: Net out the big debris ASAP, brush the walls and floor, run your pump 24/7 until water clears, and test your chemistry. Phosphates likely spiked — consider a phosphate remover if you see any green tint starting.

March 2026

Phosphates: the hidden fuel behind every green pool

You test your chlorine. You balance pH. But did you check your phosphates? These invisible nutrients are algae's favorite food. When phosphate levels climb above 500 ppb, you're basically laying out a welcome mat for algae — no matter how much chlorine you throw at it.

The fix: Test your phosphates with a kit (or bring us a water sample). If they're high, use a phosphate remover. It's cheap insurance that makes all your other chemicals work harder.

March 2026

You're probably not brushing your pool enough

Run the pump, test the water, add chemicals — done, right? Wrong. Algae clings to pool walls and floor in a slimy biofilm that your filter can't touch. Brushing breaks up that biofilm so your sanitizer can actually kill it.

How often? In peak summer, brush 2-3 times per week minimum. In spring as temps rise, start now. Use a quality brush (nylon for plaster, stainless for vinyl) and hit the walls, steps, and corners where flow is weakest.
